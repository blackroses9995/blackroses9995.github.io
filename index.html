Arcane Armaments

  • Name: Arcane Armaments
  • Date: October 2018
  • Category: Graphic Design

Arcane Armaments was I project I completed in my New Media Digital Design II class that followed the creation of a logo from object to implementation over the course of three weeks.

We began by selecting a real world object and taking pictures of it at various angles. From there we selected four pictures to move forward with. Things I considered when selecting my pictures were lighting, overall angle, and more specifically tilt.

From there we were tasked with making digital drawings of each of our chosen pictures. For me, with drawing not being one of my strong suits, I was proud of what I was able to accomplish for my first time using a drawing tablet.

From there we were asked to do four different types of "coloring" to explore the structure of the drawing. The first was to color it all black and see if it passed the silhouette test, (i.e. recognizing the drawing as a hammer without ant details). The other three for explorations in highlighting certain aspects of the hammer (e.g. the handle).

From there we were told to pick one of our drawings and turn it into a logo. That's all they gave us. I drew many rough sketches, all very complex before I sat down and looked at other logos and saw that they were all so simple. Realizing I was way over thinking it I came up with a black and white, simple hammer. From there I thought about the context of my logo and arrived at the point of where I changed my entire view of the hammer...and simplified it even further down to two basic rectangles.

I had the idea of making the logo for a place called Arcane Armaments for a fantasy blacksmith for a D&D type world. The top photo is the original presented concepted and the bottom one is the adjusted one after receiving some feedback. Some of the changes included removing the inverted, double A's because people mistook them has the Jewish Star, also enlarging the A being hammered down, thickening the lines below the words, adding swings marks on the hammer, and finally adding an additional rectangle to give the hammer more definition.

  • Name: Jacqueline Casey Inspired Poster
  • Date: December 2018
  • Category: Graphic Design

In a class we were each tasked with choosing a designer or typographer to research their life and their style and then to create a poster in their regards. The topic of the poster was to image that your designer was coming to campus to give a talk on a topic of our choosing. The poster also had to place an emphasis on typography in same way. I choose to do "Navigating Design" and in typical Casey fashion created an intriguing and symbolic puzzle to figure out...a maze. Design is a complex process so the maze only has one entry point but multiple exits to show that the design process is not always straight forward nor does it always work out the way we think it will. The entire maze was crafted using a grid system, as Casey was a well known Swiss Designer.

neopets for Mobile

  • Name: neopets for Mobile
  • Date: December 2018
  • Category: App Design
  • Prototype Link: Adobe XD Prototype

neopets for mobile was a short, high-fidelity prototype done for an app design project originally for class, and then later modified for fun.

To begin the project I started with some concept sketches of how I would overcome the peculiar design challenges that neopets on mobile would have. Navigation was the first big challenge I attempted to conquer. In the actual website, noepets, uses dropdown menus for advanced navigation through the site. To solve this I combined the use of a traditional side drawer for basic navigation along side the use of a dial for more advanced navigational processes.

The next few tasks to sort out were the pet information and the display of items throughout the website. Traditionally the neopet information was displayed on the left side of the screen but with the app design I moved it to the right side drawer. For displaying items I choose a "below the box" method and color coded the rarities of the items.

The first screen is simply the splash screen that will be displayed on loading the app or loading pages from place to place within the app. It consists of the neopets logo and their iconic yellow background with stars.

The second screen is home screen for neopets. It was my goal to keep the design as close as possible to the one on neopets website. I kept many of the featured home items and translated them, quite successfully, into the app design.

The third screen here is what the neopet information would look like from the right side drawer. It features all of the key statistics found on their individual pet pages. Additionally, below it is the neofriends portion.

This fourth screen is the main navigation, closely copied over from the main website. Each category that can go beyond the basic navigation has a yellow plus sign that will open up the fifth screen.

This fifth screen opens advanced navigation in the form of a radial menu. The one shown here is for exploring the world of neopets. Each advanced navigation dial will bring up the prospective icons of each item (in this case place) and as you spin the dial around the icon and name will show up in the center. From their they can either double tap the center icon or the button to go to the selected place.

Finally, the 6th screen I made is a quick representation of what the map of places would look like. There was a great amount of deliberation on my end on how best to present it and in the end I choose a very Google-Map esque approach because neopets has hidden places you can find on the worlds. The presented map would be able to pinch and zoom like a normal map and tapping on either the sign post or the physical building would bring users into the location. Additionally, since this allows for so much extra space, I reserved a spot for ad placements because neopets generates a large portion of their revenue from these advertisements.

Ember

  • Name: Ember
  • Date: January 2017
  • Category: Graphic Design

Ember was one of my very first beginning Adobe Photoshop projects that I did to help familiarize myself with the product. In completing Ember I learned the basics of where 90% of the tools are and what they do, the high importance of managing your layers properly, clipping, and blending hue, saturation, and lighting aspects between layers.

Featured here are the five (5) free assets that I used to create Ember. I used the nebula as the main point of the background and added approximately 14 layers of lighting, hue, and saturation adjustments to create just the background effect. Then I clipped the model and added her to the background and adjusted lighting issues most noticeably on her face and hair. I then added in the firework sparks and blended them in. And finally I clipped certain portions of each fire and added them to the pictures.

The top two photos are just lighting and saturation that I was playing around with and made some pretty cool effects that I like to share. The bottom left was the first "finished" presentation of Ember. After getting some feedback by friends I edited her to be the bottom right image. Overall Ember was a great exploration into learning how to use a software and I am extremely proud of her as a project.

iago

  • Name: iago
  • Date: March 2019
  • Category: App Design
  • Prototype Link: Lo-Fi Prototype Hi-Fi Prototype

iago is an ongoing project that I am currently working on. I am currently developing the high fidelity prototype of iago and will publish the results when available.

The whole basis of iago is creating your dream vacation for your budget, all with an easy to use app. iago is a multi-step app that will include 5 major steps in it's creation:

  1. Developing the project website and basic design sketches.
  2. Development of the Lo-Fi prototype.
  3. Evaluation of the Lo-Fi prototype.
  4. Development of the Hi-Fi prototype.
  5. Evaluation of the Hi-Fi prototype and applying any tweaks.

You can view the developmental website of iago by clicking here. A complete evaluation of the problem as well as explanations of what to prototype are explained. Additionally, you can view my "archives", which is essentially my hand written notes and logic for iago, by clicking here.

This, and the above photo, are examples of what the low fidelity prototype of iago looks like. You can view all of the screens of iago by clicking here.

Additionally, you can use the InVision prototype and walk through iago's base structure by clicking here.

Here's a quick snapshot of iago's lo-fi features:

  • simple bottom 3-tab navigation
    • with an expanded top 2-tab navigation under the "trips" section.
  • a system that allows for the creation of "trip profiles" that can be reused in trip planning, cutting down time
  • a step-by-step planning wizard that walks users through planning their perfect trip
  • a uniquely generated list of trips that match your preferences at the end of the wizard
  • the ability to see why trips are a good fit or not for your vacation
  • the ability to save trips and edit them later on

This photo shows examples of what the high fidelity prototype of iago looks like. You can view all of the screens of the high fidelity iago prototype by clicking here.

Additionally, you can use the InVision prototype and walk through iago by clicking here.

An interesting fact about the hi-fi prototype for iago is that I have never done designs for iOS before. In fact, I've never so much as held an iPhone, so this was a particularly interesting challenge for me; going from material design to the standard of iOS, which is much more flat and widely different from anything I had ever designed before. I mostly found that I had to pick different icons, dialog boxes, and get rid of what I call the "filler shapes". One example of this being I dropped the light blue color surrounding the progress meter in the wizard, and just kept it a simple white, in style with typical iOS design. I also made the section headers and other titles much, much larger, as I saw it was a normative in iOS designs that I found in my research.
